Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and urad dal batter. Light, golden, and delicious, dosas are often enjoyed with coconut chutney, sambar, and sometimes filled with spiced potato filling (as in masala dosa). It’s a beloved breakfast or dinner option, known for its taste, texture, and versatility.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal
  • Fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps for making

  1. Soak 2 cups rice and 1/2 cup urad dal with 1 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional) for 4–6 hours.
  2. Grind everything to a smooth batter. Add water if needed to get a pouring consistency.
  3. Ferment the batter overnight or for 8–12 hours in a warm place until it doubles in volume.
  4. Stir the batter, add salt, and pour a ladleful onto a hot non-stick or cast-iron tawa. Spread in a circular motion to make a thin layer.
  5. Drizzle oil around the edges, cook until golden and crisp. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
