Poori

Poori is a popular deep-fried Indian bread that is crisp on the outside and soft on the inside. Made with whole wheat flour, it is a staple in many Indian breakfasts and festive meals. Pooris puff up beautifully when fried and are best served hot with potato curry, chana masala, or sweet dishes like halwa. Their golden color and irresistible texture make them a favorite across all age groups.

Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Oil (for frying)

Steps for making

  1. In a bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt. Add water little by little and knead into a firm dough.
  2. Let the dough rest for 15â€“20 minutes covered with a cloth.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one into a small circle (not too thin).
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan. Once hot, gently slide the rolled dough into the oil.
  5. Fry until the poori puffs up and turns golden. Flip and fry the other side. Serve hot with curry or chutney.
